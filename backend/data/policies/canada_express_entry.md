# Canada Express Entry - Immigration Policy Guide

## Overview
Canada's Express Entry is a points-based immigration system managing applications for three federal economic immigration programs. It is considered one of the most transparent and efficient immigration systems globally.

## Programs Under Express Entry

### 1. Federal Skilled Worker Program (FSWP)
**Target:** Skilled workers with foreign work experience
- Minimum 1 year continuous skilled work experience in last 10 years
- Language: Minimum CLB 7 in all abilities
- Education: Post-secondary credential (assessed via ECA)
- Must score 67/100 on FSW selection factors

### 2. Federal Skilled Trades Program (FSTP)
**Target:** Skilled tradespeople
- 2 years work experience in skilled trade
- Meet job requirements for that trade
- Valid job offer OR certificate of qualification from Canadian authority

### 3. Canadian Experience Class (CEC)
**Target:** Temporary workers/students already in Canada
- 1 year skilled work experience in Canada within last 3 years
- Work experience gained while authorized to work
- Language: CLB 7 (NOC 0/A) or CLB 5 (NOC B)

## Comprehensive Ranking System (CRS)
Maximum points: 1,200

### Core CRS Factors (max 600 points with spouse, 500 without)
- **Age:** Maximum points at 20-29 years
- **Education:** Up to 150 points for PhD
- **Language:** Up to 136 points for CLB 10+
- **Work Experience:** Up to 80 points for 5+ years

### Additional Factors (max 600 points)
- **Provincial Nomination:** 600 points (guaranteed invitation)
- **Job Offer:** 50-200 points depending on NOC level
- **Canadian Education:** 15-30 points
- **French Language:** Up to 50 additional points
- **Sibling in Canada:** 15 points

## Processing Times
- Express Entry profile: Immediate assessment
- Invitation to Apply (ITA): Regular draws (every 2 weeks typically)
- Permanent Residence application: 6 months (80% of applications)

## Key Dates & Draw Patterns
- Draws typically every 2 weeks
- Category-based draws for specific occupations (STEM, healthcare, trades, French)
- Cut-off scores vary: typically 450-550 CRS for general draws

## Tips for Success
1. **Maximize language scores** - Each IELTS band above 7 adds significant points
2. **Get provincial nomination** - Adds 600 points, essentially guaranteeing ITA
3. **Consider Canadian education** - Adds points and helps with settlement
4. **French language bonus** - Even basic French adds points
5. **Prepare documents early** - ECA, police certificates take time

## Common Rejection Reasons
- Misrepresentation of work experience
- Inadequate language proof
- Incomplete police certificates
- Medical inadmissibility
- Insufficient proof of funds
