# Australia Skilled Migration - Immigration Policy Guide

## Overview
Australia's skilled migration program is points-based, similar to Canada, with multiple pathways to permanent residence. The program targets skilled workers in occupations where Australia has labor shortages.

## Skilled Visa Categories

### Subclass 189 - Skilled Independent
- **Type:** Permanent, points-tested
- **Sponsor:** None required
- **Occupation List:** Medium and Long-term Strategic Skills List (MLTSSL)
- **Points:** 65 minimum (competitive invitations at 70-80+)

### Subclass 190 - Skilled Nominated
- **Type:** Permanent, state-nominated
- **Sponsor:** State/Territory government
- **Occupation List:** State-specific lists
- **Points:** 65 minimum (includes 5 nomination points)

### Subclass 491 - Skilled Work Regional
- **Type:** Provisional (5 years), pathway to 191 PR
- **Sponsor:** State or family member in regional area
- **Occupation List:** Expanded lists
- **Points:** 65 minimum (includes 15 nomination points)

### Subclass 482 - Temporary Skill Shortage
- **Type:** Temporary (2-4 years)
- **Sponsor:** Employer
- **Path to PR:** Through 186 after 2-3 years

## Skills Assessment
Before applying, skills must be assessed by relevant authority:

| Occupation | Assessing Authority |
|------------|---------------------|
| ICT/IT | ACS |
| Engineering | Engineers Australia |
| Accounting | CPA/ICAA/IPA |
| Healthcare | Various (AHPRA, etc.) |
| Trades | TRA |
| General | VETASSESS |

### ACS (Australian Computer Society)
- Requires related bachelor's degree
- OR diploma + 5 years experience
- Deducts 2-6 years from work experience (skill period)
- 60 business days processing

## Expression of Interest (EOI)

### SkillSelect System
1. Create SkillSelect account
2. Submit EOI with points claim
3. Wait for invitation (ITA)
4. Invitation valid 60 days

### Invitation Rounds
- Monthly rounds
- Occupation ceilings apply
- Higher points = earlier invitation
- Pro-rata occupations may need 85+ points

## Processing Times (2024)
- 189: 6-12 months
- 190: 6-12 months
- 491: 6-12 months
- 482 Short-term: 1-2 months
- 482 Medium-term: 1-3 months

## State Nomination Strategies

### New South Wales
- High points threshold
- Sydney is competitive
- Strong tech sector

### Victoria
- Melbourne has strong demand
- Slightly more accessible than NSW
- Regular invitation rounds

### Queensland
- Growing tech hub (Brisbane)
- Often lower thresholds
- Regional areas prioritized

### South Australia
- Very immigration-friendly
- Lower thresholds
- Good for state nomination

### Western Australia
- Mining and resources focus
- Lower competition for some occupations

## Path to Citizenship
1. Permanent residence (189/190)
2. 4 years residence (1 year as PR)
3. Citizenship test
4. Good character
5. No more than 12 months outside Australia in 4 years

### Steps
1. Get 482 visa (employer sponsors)
2. Work 2-3 years for sponsor
3. Apply for 186 (Transition stream)
4. Become permanent resident

### Requirements for 186
- Same employer for 2-3 years
- Positive skills assessment
- Under 45 years
- Competent English (IELTS 6)

## Key Considerations

### Genuine Temporary Entrant (GTE)
- Student visas require GTE
- Assess intent to return home
- Consider ties to home country

### Health Requirements
- Medical examination required
- Some conditions may affect eligibility
- Health waiver possible in some cases

### Character Requirements
- Police certificates from all countries lived 12+ months
- No significant criminal record

## Common Issues
- Skills assessment deductions (ACS)
- Occupation ceiling reached
- Points inflation (needing 85+ for some occupations)
- State nomination competition
- Processing delays
