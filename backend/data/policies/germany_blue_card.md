# Germany EU Blue Card - Immigration Policy Guide

## Overview
The EU Blue Card is a work and residence permit for highly qualified non-EU citizens. Germany issues the most Blue Cards in the EU, making it a premier destination for skilled professionals, especially in tech.

## Eligibility Requirements

### 1. Educational Qualification
- University degree (Bachelor's or higher)
- Degree must be recognized in Germany (check anabin database)
- Or: Comparable qualification through 5 years professional experience (for IT sector)

### 2. Job Offer Requirements
- Employment contract or binding job offer
- Job must match your qualification
- Full-time position (or at least 20 hours for specific cases)

### 3. Salary Thresholds (2024)
- **Standard professions:** €45,300 gross annual salary
- **Shortage occupations:** €41,041 gross annual salary

Shortage occupations include:
- IT and software development
- Natural sciences
- Mathematics
- Engineering
- Medicine (except dentistry)

### 4. Other Requirements
- No threats to public order or security
- Valid travel document
- Health insurance coverage

## Key Advantages

### Fast-Track to Permanent Residence
- **21 months** with B1 German language
- **27 months** without German proficiency
(Compared to 5 years for regular work permits)

### EU Mobility
- After 12 months, can move to another EU country for Blue Card
- Accumulated residence counts toward EU long-term residence

### Family Benefits
- Spouse gets immediate unrestricted work permit
- Children receive residence permit
- Family can apply together

### Job Flexibility
- Can change employers with notification during first 2 years
- No restrictions after 2 years

## Application Process

### From Outside Germany
1. Gather documents (degree, job offer, insurance)
2. Apply at German embassy/consulate
3. Processing: 1-3 months
4. Receive Blue Card upon arrival

### From Within Germany (if already on valid visa)
1. Apply at local Foreigners' Authority (Ausländerbehörde)
2. May need to convert current status
3. Processing: 1-2 months

## Required Documents
- Valid passport
- Completed application form
- University degree (with recognition/apostille)
- Employment contract showing salary
- Health insurance proof
- Passport photos (biometric)
- Proof of accommodation (sometimes)

## Processing Fees
- Application fee: €75-100
- Degree recognition: Varies (€100-200 typically)

## Path to Citizenship
1. Blue Card: 4 years maximum (usually 2-3)
2. Settlement Permit (PR): After 21-27 months
3. Citizenship: After 6-8 years total residence, B1 German, citizenship test

## Important Notes

### 30% Tax Ruling Does Not Apply
Unlike Netherlands, Germany does not have special tax benefits for expats. However:
- Income splitting benefits for married couples
- Many deductions available

### Language Considerations
- Workplace: Many tech jobs are English-speaking
- Daily life: German helps significantly
- PR path: B1 German accelerates PR
- Citizenship: B1 German required

## Tech Industry Specifics
- Berlin, Munich, Hamburg main tech hubs
- Many international startups operate in English
- Strong demand for software engineers, data scientists
- IT experience can substitute for formal degree

## Common Issues
- Degree recognition delays
- Salary below threshold (negotiate!)
- Qualification-job mismatch
- Processing delays at overloaded embassies

## Citation
Source: Federal Office for Migration and Refugees (BAMF), German Skilled Immigration Act (Fachkräfteeinwanderungsgesetz), EU Blue Card Directive 2021/1883.
