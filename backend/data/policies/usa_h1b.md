# USA H-1B Visa - Immigration Policy Guide

## Overview
The H-1B is a non-immigrant visa allowing US employers to temporarily employ foreign workers in specialty occupations. It's the primary work visa for skilled professionals but is subject to an annual lottery due to high demand.

## Annual Cap & Lottery

### Regular Cap
- 65,000 visas annually
- For employers generally

### Master's Cap
- Additional 20,000 visas
- For those with US Master's or higher
- Entered first, rejected applications go to regular cap

### Cap-Exempt Employers
Not subject to lottery:
- Universities and affiliated research organizations
- Non-profit research organizations
- Government research organizations

### Lottery Statistics
- FY2024: ~780,000 registrations for 85,000 slots
- Selection rate: ~11%
- Multiple registrations (different employers) increase odds

## Specialty Occupation Requirements

### Definition
Job requiring:
1. Theoretical and practical application of specialized knowledge
2. At least a bachelor's degree (or equivalent) in specific specialty

### Common Qualifying Fields
- IT/Computer Science
- Engineering
- Finance/Accounting
- Medicine/Healthcare
- Architecture
- Science

## Eligibility Criteria

### For the Employee
- Bachelor's degree or higher (or equivalent)
- Degree must be in related specialty
- Or: 12 years progressive experience (3 years = 1 year college)

### For the Employer
- Must file petition on behalf of employee
- Pay prevailing wage or actual wage (whichever higher)
- Labor Condition Application (LCA) certification

## Timeline

### H-1B Cap Calendar
- **March 1-20:** Registration period
- **Late March:** Lottery selection announced
- **April 1 - June 30:** File petition
- **October 1:** Earliest start date

### Processing Times
- Regular: 3-6 months
- Premium Processing: 15 calendar days (additional $2,805)

## Duration & Extensions
- Initial: 3 years
- Extension: 3 years (6 total)
- Beyond 6 years: If I-140 approved or labor certification pending

## Costs

### USCIS Fees
| Fee Type | Amount |
|----------|--------|
| Registration | $215 |
| I-129 Petition | $780 |
| ACWIA Training Fee | $750-1,500 |
| Fraud Prevention | $500 |
| Premium Processing | $2,805 |

*Most fees paid by employer*

### Prevailing Wage
- Determined by DOL for occupation and location
- Example: Software Developer, San Francisco: ~$130,000+
- Must be at least Level 1 wage for area

## Portability (AC21)
After 180 days on approved H-1B:
- Can transfer to new employer
- New employer files their own petition
- Can start working upon receipt notice

## Path to Green Card

### Typical Path
1. H-1B Status
2. PERM Labor Certification (6-12 months)
3. I-140 Immigrant Petition (6-12 months)
4. I-485 Adjustment of Status / Consular Processing

### Wait Times (varies by country of birth)
- India: 10+ years for EB-2/EB-3
- China: 5-10 years for EB-2/EB-3
- Other countries: Current or 1-2 years

### H-1B Beyond 6 Years
If green card in process:
- Can extend H-1B in 1-year or 3-year increments
- Until green card approved

## Spouse (H-4) Rules

### Work Authorization
- H-4 EAD available if H-1B holder has:
  - Approved I-140, OR
  - H-1B extended beyond 6 years under AC21
- Allows any employment
- Must be renewed

### Without EAD
- Cannot work
- Can study
- Can volunteer

## Key Restrictions

### Employer Tied
- Must work for petitioning employer
- Changing jobs requires new petition
- Gap in authorization risks status

### Dual Intent
- Unlike most non-immigrant visas
- Can have immigration intent
- Can pursue green card simultaneously

## Alternative Options

### If Not Selected in Lottery

**O-1 Extraordinary Ability**
- No cap, no lottery
- For those with extraordinary achievements
- Tech criteria: GitHub stars, speaking, patents

**L-1 Intracompany Transfer**
- Work 1 year at foreign office
- Transfer to US office
- No lottery

**Cap-Exempt Employment**
- Universities, research organizations
- No lottery but specific employers only

## Common Issues

### RFE (Request for Evidence)
Common reasons:
- Specialty occupation challenge
- Beneficiary qualifications
- Employer-employee relationship

### Denial Trends
- USCIS more stringent since 2017
- Higher RFE rates for IT consulting
- Importance of detailed job descriptions
